# ICP Competition Density — Direct Competitors per Pool

Estimates blend Stack Overflow 2024 BR, LinkedIn Talent Insights, GeekHunter/Coodesh, Toptal/Upwork filters, Workana/99Freelas, and ABRADi/Abracom rosters. Numbers are order-of-magnitude *direct* competitors chasing the same pool.

## A. Recruiter BR (PJ / CLT remoto)

- **Direct competitors:** ~25–40k BR full-stack devs with PHP+JS, of which ~8–12k are mid-pleno actively visible to BR tech recruiters (LinkedIn Open-to-Work + GeekHunter/Coodesh/Revelo).
- **Archetype:** "Pleno bootcamp-trained Laravel/React, 3–5 yrs, CLT-saudoso, generic GitHub, anchor R$8–14k." Recruiter sees 80 of these per req.
- **Differentiation:** Hard. PHP+JS+Laravel+React is the *median* BR stack. César's edge (bilingual, ships solo, stitch-grade portfolio) is invisible inside ATS keyword filters — resume comes before site.
- **Differentiation ease:** **3/10**
- **Race-to-bottom risk:** **3/10** (recruiter-driven salary banding compresses upside)

## B. International remote (LATAM → US/EU)

- **Direct competitors:** ~15–25k LATAM devs on Toptal-screened / Upwork Top-Rated / EN-LinkedIn for PHP+JS+React. Upwork Top-Rated-Plus PHP+React ≈ 4–6k LATAM, plus Deel/Remote/Turing rosters.
- **Archetype:** "AR/CO senior, native-fluent EN, 6+ yrs, ex-Globant/Mercado Libre, Toptal-screened, $50–80/hr." Their EN + SaaS-product CV beat the median BR dev.
- **Differentiation:** Medium-hard. Stack is commoditized; the things that *do* matter (EN proven, US-timezone overlap, async writing, product sense) are exactly what AR/CO seniors lead on.
- **Differentiation ease:** **4/10**
- **Race-to-bottom risk:** **4/10** (Toptal gating holds a floor, but LATAM senior volume keeps pressure)

## C. Founder BR (pré-MVP a seed)

- **Direct competitors:** ~1.5–3k BR solo devs/duos credibly serving early founders. Of those, maybe **400–800 actually *talk founder*** — most pitch hours, not outcomes.
- **Archetype:** "Ex-CTO-of-failed-startup turned freelance, fractional-CTO + MVP build, R$15–30k/mês, strong on Twitter/LinkedIn-BR, weak portfolio (Notion or Linktree)." Plus Workana fee-cutters below and ex-agency mids charging premium above.
- **Differentiation:** **High.** This pool's portfolios are systematically bad — Notion, dead Behance, GitHub README-as-CV. A v2 with WhatsApp lead, EN/PT, real case studies, and stitch-grade design is rare in this exact niche. Founders value *taste + speed signal*, both of which v2 optimizes for.
- **Differentiation ease:** **8/10**
- **Race-to-bottom risk:** **7/10** (budget-aware but value velocity, less commoditized)

## D. Agência BR (overflow / white-label)

- **Direct competitors:** ~2–4k BR freelancers offering overflow. Supply is concentrated: agencies recycle ~10–20 trusted vendors each, so the *reachable* per-agency pool is ~50–150.
- **Archetype:** "Ex-agency PJ, 5–8 yrs, WordPress/Laravel/React, MEI, R$80–140/h, found via referral not website." Relationship is the entry, not the portfolio.
- **Differentiation:** Medium. Portfolio matters *less* (referrals dominate) but functions as a credibility-confirm step. Stitch-grade site signals "won't embarrass us in front of the client" — the agency's actual fear.
- **Differentiation ease:** **6/10**
- **Race-to-bottom risk:** **5/10** (per-agency rates, somewhat sticky once trusted)

## Strongest differentiation given portfolio v2 spec

**ICP C — Founder BR.** Portfolio v2's stitch-grade visuals, bilingual narrative, WhatsApp-first lead capture, and case-study-over-resume framing are *exactly* what the founder-facing competitor pool systematically lacks (Notion, dead links, hour-based pitches). In A and B the same stack puts César inside a 20k+ commoditized pool; in D the site is confirm-not-convert. Only in C does v2 convert from "nice site" to "thinks like the buyer." Recommend **C primary**, D warm-referral secondary, A/B opportunistic.
